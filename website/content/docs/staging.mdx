## What is God-Mode Staging?

TyeGit features a fully embedded instance of the **Monaco Editor** (the same editor that powers VS Code) directly in the client. This replaces the traditional "side-by-side read-only diff" with an interactive editing experience.

## Why use Hunk Staging?

Traditional Git GUIs force you to commit entire files at a time. If you have a file with a bug fix on line 10, and an unfinished experiment on line 50, committing the file pollutes your history.

With TyeGit, you can stage *only* the bug fix, commit it, and leave the experiment in your working directory.

## How to use it

### Step 1: Open the Diff Viewer
In the Left Rail (Status Panel), click on any Unstaged file that has been modified. The center pane will open the God-Mode Diff Viewer.

### Step 2: Hunk Staging and Reverting (The Arrows)
In the center margin between the left and right panes, you will see two arrows for every modified hunk:
- **`←` (Blue)**: Stage Hunk. Instantly copies that specific chunk of code from your Working Directory into your Git Index.
- **`↺` (Red)**: Revert Hunk. Surgically slices the pristine lines out of the Git Index and replaces your working directory modifications, allowing you to discard unwanted changes hunk-by-hunk.

### Step 3: Editable Index (Manual Staging)
Sometimes an arrow isn't precise enough. Because the Left Pane is a real editor, you can literally click inside it and start typing! 
You can manually delete lines, fix typos, or format code *directly inside the staging area* without touching your working directory files.
When you are done editing, click the **SAVE TO INDEX (STAGE)** button in the top right.

### Step 4: Fast Workflow Shortcuts
- **Stage All**: Press `Ctrl+S` anywhere in the app to instantly stage all modified files.
- **Quick Commit**: When typing your commit message, press `Ctrl+Enter` (or `Cmd+Enter` on Mac) to commit immediately without touching your mouse!

## Oops, I made a mistake! (Soft Reset)
Just committed something and realized you forgot a file? Or noticed a typo right after committing? 
In the Status Panel, click the **Undo Last Commit** (`Undo2` icon) button next to the Commit button. 
This will perform a `git reset --soft HEAD~1`, instantly undoing your commit and placing your files right back in the Staged section so you can fix them.

## Tips

> **Perfect Commits**: Use hunk staging to separate your massive "end of day" working directory state into 3 or 4 small, atomic, perfectly scoped commits. Reviewers will thank you.
